#PuertoRico

Get a glimpse of Puerto Rico’s vibrant culture, breathtaking landscapes, and unforgettable moments. From golden beaches and colorful colonial streets to mouthwatering dishes and flora-fauna, this photo gallery captures the heart and soul of the island—one snapshot at a time.

El Morro

A historic 16th-century fortress offering sweeping views and timeless charm.

Icacos Beach

A small uninhabited island known for its turquoise waters, snorkeling spots, and peaceful atmosphere.

Tasty Food

Puerto Rican cuisine blends Spanish, African, and Taíno influences—flavorful dishes like mofongo, lechón, and pastelillos are local favorites.

The Coquí

A tiny frog with a mighty voice, the coquí is an iconic symbol of Puerto Rican wildlife and pride.

Bad Bunny

Global music icon and proud Boricua, Bad Bunny brings Puerto Rican culture to the world stage.

La Amapola

Puerto Rico’s national flower, the amapola is a bright red hibiscus that symbolizes beauty and resilience.

Flamboyán Tree

Known for its fiery red-orange blossoms, the flamboyán is a beloved symbol of Puerto Rican summers and natural beauty.
